Mechanical Design.
Our mechanical design process focused primarily on fast prototyping, and robustness for critical systems. With this philosophy, we ensured that the design of the robot's drive train and mechanical structure were the most reliably constructed parts, so that they could best serve as the platform on top of which to build out the rest of the system.

Drive Train

Our drive train used four 4.25" Omni wheels to enable simple to implement movement in all directions. Since our movement design involved sliding along the arena walls only in cardinal direction, this drive train design provided the simplest solution to full arena movement.

Chassis

 Our robot's chassis was constructed out of laser-cut plywood and acrylic. These materials were both strong, lightweight, and easy to work with. Three, identical horizontal framing pieces created three levels within the robot to mount stuff to. Each of these platforms were then spaced by small, interlocking spacers, which connected at the corners of the platforms.

Launcher

The launcher assembly consisted of a ball hopper and a track, above which spun a flywheel driven by a small but powerful RC motor. Since the motor spun so fast, we were able to have success with a fairly low-mass flywheel.

Since this subsystem did not need to support any other parts of the robot, and benefited from being able to shift and adapt its construction at will, we made it almost entirely out of scrap materials hot glued together. Initially, tape was used to secure the placement of each piece, then hot clue was used to stick it there. When parts needed to be moved around, we could easily just use a hot air gun to reheat some of the glue, and move pieces into place.

The ball hopper was capable of holding six balls, the maximum allowable in one reload. A small servo at the bottom prevented balls from rolling all the way into the flywheel until ready. When the robot decided it was time to shoot, it let the servo rotate out of the way long enough for one ball to get launched by the wheel.
